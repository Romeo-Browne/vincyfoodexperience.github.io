Fried Bakes

Traced back in Vincentian culture since colonial times, fried bakes has always been a part of Vincentian dietary options. Its primary ingredients includes flour, yeast/baking powder, sugar, and salt. It is prepared by frying it in oil (corn, vegetable etc.) until that desired golden look and or texture is observed. Bakes can be prepared as a meal for anytime of the day. It is mostly prepared as a meal with saltfish or fried jack fish in St. Vincent and the Grenadines as breakfast option.

Fried Fish

Vincy offerings of fried fish are almost always exceptionally delicious. The reason is because of how it is prepared. the fish is soaked in lime, salt, and water during preparation; they are then marinated or stuffed with savory Caribbean seasonings; and they are fried in soft, home-seasoned breadcrumbs. What is most intresting us that fried fish màkes up our national dish.

Pealau

A traditional rice dish of the West Indies. It is a one pot Vincy favourite.Main ingredients are meat (usually chickenor beef), rice, pigeon peas or cowpeas, coconut milk and sugar; various vegetables and spices are optional ingredients.Spices used in the dish include cloves, cumin and coriander. The meat is caramelised and the other ingredients are then added one by one, resulting in a dark brown stew.

Sour Sap Juice

SOURSOP Juice is a tropical drink that is both super nutritious and super delicious. Most people I know who have tried this exotic juice have fallen in love with it. Growing up in Saint Vincent it was always a treat to have soursop juice with dinner on a Sunday. I remember my mother making soursop juice two ways – with lime or with milk. At she only added lime and brown sugar to her Soursop Juice to make a cool and refreshing beverage. At other times, she made Soursop Juice by adding spices and sweetened condensed milk to make a rich and creamy drink. No matter how she made her Soursop Juice, however, they were both equally special and delicious. v
